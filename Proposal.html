Project Proposal

Problem Description

The general question we have is primarily based around the medical field with the central question being “is there an easier way to reduce the burden of seeking medical care?”.Based on the article made by USC it stated that ”38% of U.S. adults had skipped some form of health care (i.e., a test, treatment, visit, or prescription fill) in the past year due to financial burden”(USC EMHA Online,2023). One major information technology problem which may be spurring on this financial burden is the lack of easily accessible and accurate information online thus reducing the need for unnecessary hospital visits and undue financial hardship. Previously this was done during covid, but with the easing of covid policies so was telemedical services were also reduced as they were less financially viable.

A previous attempt at utilizing an online health platform that is designed to help users evaluate and understand their health symptoms is WebMD . This website allows users to input the symptoms an individual is experiencing, ask questions to narrow down the possibilities, produce possible conditions, and read information on possible causes and symptoms. It provides general content and suggestions based on the user's symptoms without taking into account an individual's medical history. However, there is potential for improvements like more customizability features for the users, incorporating the context of a user's history, and providing more relevant information. Overall, WebMD has attempted to provide individuals with an online space to seek guidance in understanding health indicators.

Proposed Application

This web application allows users to obtain accurate online symptom diagnosis through telemedicine services without having to go to the hospital and make an appointment with a doctor, allowing them to quickly understand their disease and the steps they should take to help themselves urgently when necessary. This feature helps, especially for people who are financially strapped, so that they do not have to skip healthcare due to their financial situation, thus increasing their health. Users can enter their symptoms and receive the Symptom Monitor's diagnosis of possible illnesses, next-step treatment recommendations, and search for useful assistance resources. Our platform will also allow users to search for information about hospitals near them. If it is a minor illness, users can go to drugstores such as CVS to buy medicines according to the recommendations. If it is a serious disease, users can quickly check the surrounding hospitals and walk in or make an appointment for treatment. At the same time, users can check recommended government rescue plans and other social assistance to obtain financial and material support.

The information that the users will be viewing is related to the user's given symptoms such as valuable suggestions or insights into what they may be experiencing. As well as users will be viewing content related to hospitals near their zip code. This will be from an external data source where it contains the facility name, address, city/town, and state of where the hospital is located near the given user. This data is provided from Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services. As well as including possible resources about available government programs related to healthcare and assistance. There will be a wide range of information that the user can digest and utilize for their well-being.

First, the most important feature of our web app, 'Symptom Quiz', allows users to describe their symptoms and get medical advice in a quiz. In addition, we designed the 'Find a Hospital' function to help users quickly find nearby medical resources, which is also crucial for emergencies. Secondly, we provide 'Daily Diary/Daily Health Check-in', which allows users to record and track health data to help them with long-term health management. In addition, we will also design a 'Resources page' to provide people with information on government funding programs and social assistance to support patients with financial difficulties. Finally, potential features we are also considering are 'Doctor Finder' and 'Virtual Doctor Consultation'. We will further improve medical support and user experience by providing the function of finding a doctor and making an appointment for consultation online.
